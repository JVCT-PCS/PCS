2026 Edition Authority: JTR Ch. 5 · AFI 24-602 V4 · DTR Part IV Published by: PPA HQ, JBSA Randolph AFB Effective: 15 May 2026

⚠ Important Disclaimer

This handbook is a condensed training and reference guide for Air Force personal property counselors. It DOES NOT encompass all possible counseling situations and is NOT intended to replace current regulations. Always consult the JTR, AFI 24-602 V4, DTR Part IV, and current amendments for authoritative guidance. Regulations change frequently — verify currency before advising members.

📖 Foreword

Counseling is one of the most important and challenging tasks Traffic Management professionals perform. The success or failure of each member's move depends in large part on how well they are counseled. Customers rely on trained counselors to inform and prepare them for their upcoming move.

This handbook follows the format of DD Form 1797 (military) and DD Form 2278 (PPM). Each numbered section heading corresponds to the appropriate counseling form block. Parts I–VIII correspond to DD Form 1797; Part X corresponds to DD Form 2278.

Topics are primarily oriented to Air Force military personnel entitlements, but DoD civilian entitlements are also addressed. Civilian-specific notes are clearly marked throughout.

For questions on excess cost adjudication: hqppa.ecaf-adj@us.af.mil

I

Household Goods (HHG)

Corresponds to DD Form 1797, Part II

DD FORM 1797 PART II

ℹ Scope

Unless otherwise noted, all JTR Chapter 5 and AFI 24-602 V4 references apply to both Air Force military members and civilian employees. Review orders carefully to determine the member's or employee's allowances. If a conflict is suspected, return orders to the member for resolution with MPF before counseling.

JTR 051301 AFI 24-602 V4 DTR Part IV Ch. 401

The Government's HHG transportation obligation is limited to the cost of transporting the member's maximum HHG weight allowance in one lot between authorized places at the Government's "Best Value" cost. Exceptions to the one-lot concept include UB, NTS, and PPM shipments.

✅ Counseling Tip: Overseas Administrative Weight Limits

Overseas administrative weight limitations apply and may be lower than the full JTR allowance. Exceptions require member request, approval prior to the effective date of PCS, and inclusion in orders. Use SF Form 1200 when the original GBL has already been processed. Overseas weight limits do NOT apply to members separating or retiring from an overseas area. (JTR 051402 · AFI 24-602 V4, Para 3.2)

📚 PBP&E and Gun Safes

JTR 051304-A1 AFI 24-602 V4, Para 3.1.3 & Attachment 6 JTR 051304-C

PBP&E — Professional Books, Papers, and Equipment

  • NOT charged against the member's authorized weight allowance
  • Maximum: 2,000 lbs net (member) + 500 lbs net (spouse)
  • Must be necessary for performance of official duties — member's declaration is certification
  • Must be declared at counseling and clearly identified on inventory (PRO, PBP&E, S-PRO, M-PRO, etc.)
  • Constructive weight: 7 lbs per cubic foot if scales not available; TSP must note cubic size per carton
  • TSP failing to ID PBP&E at origin: destination PPSO may inspect upon delivery if still in original packed cartons
  • After-the-fact declarations require indisputable intent; document on DD Form 619, DD 1299, GBL, or inventory
Example — PBP&E Weight Credit

E-7 member ships 12,000 lbs total. PBP&E declared: 1,800 lbs member, 400 lbs spouse. E-7 with dependents allowance = 11,000 lbs.

Chargeable HHG = 12,000 − 1,800 − 400 = 9,800 lbs — under the 11,000 lb allowance. No excess cost.

Chargeable Weight: 9,800 lbs ✓ WITHIN ALLOWANCE

Gun Safes

  • Transported same way and to same location as HHG
  • NOT charged against the authorized HHG weight allowance
  • Maximum: 500 lbs net weight additional allowance
  • Remove all weapons and other items from safe before shipment per DTR Part IV, Ch. 401, F.5.a

💰 Excess Costs

JTR 051306-A2 AFI 24-602 V4, Para 12.3.1 JTR 051306-B Table 5-36 Item 2

⚠ Financial Liability

By signing DD Form 1299, the member agrees to pay ALL excess costs incurred. The member is financially responsible for excess weight charges when the authorized weight allowance is exceeded.

  • PPPO/JPPSO is responsible for collecting all known excess costs prior to releasing the shipment for members separating in a non-pay status
  • Once property reaches the proper destination, it cannot be held ransom for non-payment of excess costs
  • For multiple shipments: excess costs computed on the shipment resulting in the least cost to the member
  • Unauthorized items discovered at destination: member reimburses all shipping costs related to those items

⚠ Never Sign a Blank Document

Members must never sign blank inventory sheets, blank DD Forms, or any other blank documents during the move. (DTR Part IV, Ch. 401, Appendix K1, Para X.1.jj)

7-Day Spread Dates / Pickup Dates

  • 7-day spread dates apply to standard HHG shipments — NOT NTS Handling In, NTS release, or DPM shipments
  • Member identifies desired pickup date in DPS; TSP may negotiate actual pack/pickup dates during pre-move survey
  • Missed pickup or failed delivery (not beyond member's control): excess costs charged to the member (JTR 051306-F)
  • Member or authorized agent MUST be present 0800–1700 on agreed pack/load dates

✅ Required Delivery Date (RDD)

DPS displays the Government-allowed RDD. Advise customers the Desired Delivery Date (DDD) becomes the RDD when booking. Caution against driver promises of specific delivery dates — drivers follow dispatcher routing.

II

Unaccompanied Baggage (UB)

Corresponds to DD Form 1797, Part II

DD FORM 1797 PART II
JTR 051403 AFI 24-602 V4, Attachment 4, Table A4.1

⚠ EXTREMELY Expensive — Air Shipment

UB shipped by air is extremely expensive and can result in significant excess cost when weight allowances are exceeded. Counsel members thoroughly. (JTR 051306-A)

Although UB may be shipped separately from HHG, it is chargeable against the member's total JTR weight allowance. UB is typically items needed to establish a temporary residence: clothing, pots/pans, light housekeeping items, cribs, playpens, collapsible strollers — no major furnishings.

Student Baggage Storage

During annual student trip between school and member's PDS, the member may store student UB (up to 350 lbs) in the school vicinity. Storage cost limited to the cost of round-trip transportation for UB. Student travel order MUST contain a LOA for storage. (JTR 050816-D10)

✈ Methods of UB Transportation

  • CONUS-to-CONUS: Normally included with HHG as "designated items for extra pickup/delivery" on one GBL. Expedited mode authorized when HHG transit time doesn't meet UB RDD.
  • OCONUS: Normal mode is airlift. Split shipments (part air, part surface) NOT authorized for dependents or members authorized concurrent travel. Exception: host country restrictions.
  • Hard Lift Areas: 10% of full JTR weight allowance may be shipped by surface or airlift — not both (split prohibited)

✅ Counseling Tip: Valuable Items

Advise members to hand-carry cameras, jewelry, money, and pilferable items. Photograph or video valuables before shipment to assist in recovery of stolen items. (AFI 24-602 V4, Para 3.5.3.3)

⚠ NTS Is Part of HHG Weight Allowance

Property placed in NTS is chargeable against the member's JTR authorized weight allowance. (JTR 0518-B3)

📍 Location of NTS

  • In an approved commercial or government storage facility nearest to where HHGs are located on the date of issuance of PCS orders (JTR 0518-B2)
  • OCONUS PCS/retirement/separation: property normally returned to CONUS for NTS (AFI 24-602 V4, Para 6.2.1)
  • Provide member with address and telephone numbers of storage facility

Counseling Requirements for NTS

  • Advise members to keep PPSO informed of assignment status changes or tour extensions affecting NTS entitlement
  • Civilians: coordinate with personnel office for new LOA at beginning of each fiscal year
  • Appliances: drain and remove all moisture to prevent mold/mildew under long-term storage
  • Member retains a legible copy of the inventory — critically important for crosscheck upon release
  • Advise members to review and dispose of unwanted items — excess NTS weight increases excess cost exposure

✅ Withdrawal from NTS

Withdrawal from NTS as an alternative to continued storage includes local release, unpacking, and uncrating at Government expense. (JTR 0518-B6)

Weapons / Privately Owned Firearms (POF)

Corresponds to DD Form 1797, Part IV

DD FORM 1797 PART IV
JTR 051303 DTR Chapter 409

🔴 Critical — Review PPCIG Before Every Firearms Counseling

Failure to counsel correctly can result in: shipments held at customs, confiscation, fines imposed by the host country, and possible UCMJ action against the service member. Ensure DD Form 1797 is initialed when member is shipping firearms OCONUS.

🔫 CONUS Shipment of Firearms

JTR 051303 DTR Ch. 409, Para C1
  • Customers are authorized to ship all conventional, antique, and surplus military firearms within CONUS
  • Owner must be in possession of all licenses, permits, or documentation required by law
  • Prohibited: Importation of foreign and domestic semi-automatic assault weapons (DTR Ch. 409, Para B1)

✈ Shipping Firearms CONUS → OCONUS

DTR Ch. 409, Para C2
  • CBP Form 4455 (Certificate of Registration) must be executed at any CBP office before departure from the U.S.
  • Establishes that firearms were previously owned in the U.S.
  • Alternatives to CBP 4455: bill of sale, receipt, HHG inventory/packing list, or application to ship personal property with adequate description

Privately Owned Vehicles (POV)

Corresponds to DD Form 1797, Part V · Vehicles, motorcycles, and leased vehicles

DD FORM 1797 PART V
JTR 0529–0532 DTR Ch. 408 · Appendix K3 / K4 AFI 24-602 V4, Ch. 7

⚠ Review PPCIG for Every POV Counseling Session

Varied and sometimes complex restrictions (size, weight, engine size, right-hand drive, etc.) are imposed on POVs in OCONUS locations. Review PPCIG before every POV counseling session.

🚗 What Qualifies as a POV?

A motor vehicle with four or more wheels, owned or leased (long-term, 12+ months) by a service member, civilian employee, or dependent. Must be: self-propelled, licensed to travel on public highways, and designed to carry passengers or HHG. Also includes airplanes, boats, and helicopters for broad POV definition purposes. (JTR Appendix A)

Size Limitation (Measurement Tons)

⚠ 20 MT Maximum

Maximum POV size at Government expense = 20 measurement tons. Formula: (Length × Width × Height in feet) ÷ 40. Member pays excess cost when exceeded. Oversized POV for medical reasons requires MPF approval. (JTR 053001-B2a · AFI 24-602 V4, Para 7.6.3)

Example — Measurement Tons Calculation

Member wants to ship a large SUV: 16 ft long × 6.5 ft wide × 6 ft tall.

MT = (16 × 6.5 × 6) ÷ 40 = 624 ÷ 40 = 15.6 MT

15.6 MT — Within 20 MT limit ✓ No excess size cost

🔧 POV Preparation for Port Turn-In

DTR Part IV, Ch. 408, Para O DTR Part IV, Attachment K3
  • Safe operating condition with valid state inspection sticker (if required)
  • Radiator antifreeze tested to −20°F (or colder if needed)
  • Fuel: less than ¼ tank
  • All exterior surfaces and undercarriage: clean and free of soil and foreign matter
  • Check for open recalls: vinrcl.safercar.gov/vin
  • Complete and legible PCS order — all pages, amendments, numbered statements; no cut-off or blurry orders
  • Spouse turning in POV: copy of marriage certificate required
  • Anyone other than member/spouse: Power of Attorney, Letter of Authorization, or other acceptable evidence of agency
  • DD Form 788 (automobiles), DD Form 788-1 (vans), DD Form 788-2 (motorcycles) — documents condition at turn-in
  • Leased vehicle: written authority from leasing company required

Boats & Personal Watercraft

Corresponds to DD Form 1797, Part VI

DD FORM 1797 PART VI
DTR Part IV, Ch. 412 JTR 051301-A AFI 24-602 V4, Para 7.9

🔴 High Excess Cost Risk — Counsel Thoroughly

Boat shipments almost always result in excess costs, which may be very high. Strongly encourage members to consider PPM for boat movements.

⛵ Size Thresholds and Methods

Dimension LimitMaximum
Length14 feet
Width6 feet 10 inches
Height6 feet 5 inches
  • Boats/watercraft ≤ dimensions above: shipped with HHG or by PPM (canoes, kayaks, jet skis, skiffs, sculls, dinghies, rowboats included)
  • Boats > dimensions above (CONUS): shipped separately via Boat One-Time-Only (BOTO) program or PPM
  • OCONUS (including Alaska): combined boat/trailer > dimensions → BOTO or PPM
  • No size limitations for members — members may ship any size boat; civilians limited to van-moveable size (DTR Part IV, Ch. 412, Para A3 vs A4)

⚠ Cost Limitation

Boat entitlement is limited to an amount not to exceed what it would have cost to move a like weight of HHG (the member's maximum allowance, one lot, between authorized origin and destination). All excess costs are the member's responsibility. (JTR 051301-A)

BOTO Excess Cost — Important Counseling Point

  • The authorized Government cost is based on HHG rate for that weight, while BOTO is a fixed rate based on space — BOTO almost always costs more
  • The fact that the member is below the prescribed weight allowance does not reduce or eliminate BOTO excess cost
  • International BOTO: carrier also receives BOTO rate for HHG, computed on per-cwt basis (usually higher than standard HHG rate)
  • Determine excess cost as soon as BOTO rate is received; advise member immediately and provide opportunity to make other arrangements

Boat Storage

  • NTS authorized; no size limitations for members (DTR Part IV, Ch. 412, Para F1)
  • Contractor entitled to weight additive not to exceed commercial tariff — counts against member's weight allowance
  • Special packing/crating/handling: member's responsibility
  • Water storage of boats is NOT authorized at Government expense
  • Member may self-store and file for reimbursement (limited to Government cost for same weight of HHG; certified weight tickets required)

Boats as Primary Residence

Boats used as primary residence may be shipped as a mobile home IAW DTR Part IV, Ch. 407. Primary residence status must be annotated on travel authorization and transportation documents. (AFI 24-602 V4, Para 7.9.2.4)

VII

Mobile Homes

Mobile Home One-Time-Only (MOTO) Program

DTR PART IV CH. 407
JTR 0522, 0523, 0524 DTR Part IV, Ch. 407 · Attachment K5 AFI 24-602 V4

⚠ Rare — Prepare Thoroughly

Mobile home moves are rare. Review DTR Part IV, Ch. 407 and Attachment K5 ("Moving Your Mobile Home") in detail before counseling. Discuss excess costs, preparation, road worthiness, and transit storage carefully.

🏠 Key Mobile Home Counseling Points

  • Movement at Government expense subject to same cost limitation as boats/HHG — not to exceed like weight of HHG
  • Small shipment of HHG may be authorized for safety purposes (essential items)
  • Member responsible for road worthiness, permits, oversize/overweight requirements
  • TSP provides all included accessorial services (blocking, unblocking, anchoring, skirting, expando/slideout, double-wide separation/reassembly) as part of MOTO rate
  • Customer responsible: reconnect/disconnect utilities and air conditioner
  • SIT for mobile homes: approved by PPSO; number of storage days must be included in OTO request

VIII

RITA — Relocation Income Tax Allowance

Reimbursement for federal and state taxes on PCS-related reimbursements

JTR CH. 5 PART E
JTR Chapter 5, Part E AFI 24-602 V4 26 U.S.C. (Internal Revenue Code)

💵 What is RITA?

RITA (Relocation Income Tax Allowance) reimburses eligible members and civilian employees for the additional federal and, in some cases, state income taxes they must pay because most PCS-related allowances (MIHA, MALT, DLA, PPM incentive payments, etc.) are included in taxable income.

ℹ Two-Year Process

  • Year 1: Member receives a Withholding Tax Allowance (WTA) — an advance to offset taxes withheld in the year of the move
  • Year 2: Member files for RITA after filing federal tax return — reimbursement based on actual taxes paid minus WTA already received

📋 Eligibility

  • Military members with PCS orders — generally authorized RITA for moves on/after 1 Jan 2018
  • Civilian employees on PCS orders — authorized under JTR Chapter 5, Part F / Chapter 5 Part E
  • Must file federal income tax return (Form 1040) for the tax year of the move
  • Must file RITA claim through Defense Finance and Accounting Service (DFAS) via myPay or official voucher process

📅 Filing Timeline

StepTiming
WTA issued automatically with most PCS allowance paymentsDuring year of move
File federal tax returnBy April 15 of year following move
Submit RITA claim via myPay/DFASAfter filing federal return; deadline per JTR/DFAS

⚠ Taxable PCS Allowances (Not All-Inclusive)

  • DLA (Dislocation Allowance)
  • PPM incentive payment
  • MIHA (Move-In Housing Allowance) in some cases
  • TLE (Temporary Lodging Expense) above excludable amount
  • Reimbursed house-hunting trip expenses above excludable amount

Advise members to consult their base Tax Assistance Center for specific RITA calculations.

✅ Counseling Tip

Advise all members and civilian employees on PCS moves about RITA. Remind them to retain all PCS-related financial documents (W-2, LES copies, vouchers) for the two years of the RITA process. Direct to DFAS and base Tax Assistance for filing assistance.

State Tax RITA

Some states do not provide a state income tax exclusion for military PCS allowances. Members in these states may be eligible for additional RITA reimbursement for state taxes. Verify with the Tax Assistance Center — state RITA eligibility and amounts vary.

👤 Civilian Employees: JTR Chapter 5, Part F and Chapter 5 Part E — RITA is also available for civilian employees on PCS orders. Same two-year WTA/RITA process applies.

🛡 Full Replacement Value (FRV) Protection

All DoD customers are eligible for FRV protection on DoD-funded personal property shipments. Claims must be filed through the TSP first to secure FRV eligibility. Use DPS if the shipment was processed through DPS start-to-finish.

Claim TypeTSP Maximum LiabilityReference
HHG/UB claim filed with TSP within 9 months of deliveryGreater of: $10,000 per shipment OR $6.00 × net weight (max $75,000)DTR Part IV, Attachment K
Quick Claim Settlement (TSP)<$12,000; within 5 calendar days of deliveryDTR Part IV, Attachment K
MCO claim (depreciated value)No later than 2 years from delivery (after 9 months)claims.jag.af.mil

📝 Claims Process Flow

  1. At Delivery: Complete "Notification of Loss or Damage AT Delivery" form in DPS
  2. After Delivery: Complete "Notice of Loss or Damage AFTER Delivery" in DPS
  3. TSP is responsible for obtaining repair estimates (DTR Part IV, Attachment K, Para Y3c)
  4. TSP must pay, deny, or make offer within 60 days of claim receipt
  5. Member may accept in full or reject TSP's offer on a line-by-line basis
  6. If unsatisfied after 30 days: transfer claim to Military Claims Office (MCO)
  7. MCO pays depreciated value, pursues FRV with TSP; member receives difference if FRV settlement exceeds MCO payment

  • File within 2 years of delivery with: POV contractor or MCO

💎 High Value / Extraordinary Value Items

  • Advise member to obtain additional insurance through TSP or commercial company for: currency, coins, jewelry, silverware, watches, crystal, figurines, furs, art, manuscripts, collectibles, rare documents with value >$100/lb (AFI 24-602 V4, Para 3.5.3.1 and 3.5.3.2)
  • Strongly encourage PPM or hand-carry for high value items when possible
  • Encourage photography/video of valuables before shipment

ℹ Special PPM Situations

Commercial Carriers

Members may hire a commercial carrier for PPM. The contract is between the member and the 3rd party — the Government cannot intervene on the member's behalf. Advise members to research companies carefully before signing any contract.

Small Package Services (Mail/UPS/FedEx)

Actual cost reimbursement only. Receipt/invoice must include: mailing facility address, date, weight, and cost. Cost of insurance is NOT reimbursable.

RV / 5th Wheel / Camper

Member may use RV or 5th wheel to move personal property. The weight of the RV/5th wheel/camper/mobile home itself is NOT HHG and the Government will NOT pay for that weight. Member must remove personal property from the RV to obtain a valid empty weight ticket. (JTR Appendix A)

Boat via PPM

Members may PPM boats that qualify as HHG. A weight ticket indicating gross weight of the boat is required. Note: an empty weight ticket cannot be obtained for boats — only one gross weight ticket is required for incentive payment. (DTR Part IV, Ch. 412, Para A6)

ℹ Service Member Married to Civilian Employee

A civilian employee whose spouse/domestic partner is a service member retains HHG transportation and storage allowances if a PCS order is issued. They may use combined weight allowances to offset any excess, but may NOT both be paid/reimbursed for shipping the same HHG. (JTR 054303 · Comptroller General Decision B-202023, 4 Dec 1981)
