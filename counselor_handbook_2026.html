⚖️ Governing Authority

Joint Travel Regulations (JTR) — Chapter 5 governs all HHG, NTS, and PPM entitlements for Uniformed Service members. Chapter 5 Part C addresses DoD civilian PCS transportation.

DTR Part IV (Defense Transportation Regulation Part IV) — Operational procedures for personal property shipment; Appendices A–M list all current DD Forms 3151–3178 (January 2026 edition).

AFI 24-602 Volume 4 — Air Force instruction implementing DTR Part IV for Air Force and Space Force personal property programs.

MILPERSMAN 4650-010 — Navy Personnel Manual procedures for household goods.

MCO 4600.40 — Marine Corps Order for personal property and transportation.

COMDTINST M4050.6 — Coast Guard personal property management instruction.

AR 55-71 — Army Regulation for transportation of personal property.

👤 Counselor Responsibilities

The Personal Property Counselor is the first line of guidance for service members and DoD civilians during PCS, TDY, separation, or retirement moves. Key responsibilities include:

  • Verify orders and determine correct shipment entitlements based on rank, dependency status, and move type
  • Brief members on all applicable DD Forms (3151–3178 series) and ensure completion prior to shipment
  • Counsel on weight allowances, weight tickets, and consequences of excess weight
  • Advise on prohibited/restricted items, firearms (DD Form 3152), and special-handling requirements
  • Provide FRV (Full Replacement Value) coverage counseling using DD Form 3153
  • Brief PPM/DITY procedures and complete DD Form 3166 checklist
  • Ensure all POV shipments are counseled using DD Forms 3163, 3164, 3165 as applicable
  • Document all counseling in DPS (Defense Personal Property System)

💼 PBP&E — DD Form 3168

Professional Books, Papers, and Equipment (PBP&E) shipments are authorized in addition to the HHG weight allowance and do not count against it.

  • Member's PBP&E: Up to 2,000 lbs — items directly related to military duties
  • Spouse's PBP&E: Up to 500 lbs — items required for spouse's professional occupation
  • Both member and spouse must complete DD Form 3168 with a detailed itemized list
  • PBP&E must be packed separately from HHG and listed on a separate inventory
  • Counselor must review DD 3168 and ensure itemization is complete before authorizing shipment
ℹ️Examples of qualifying member PBP&E: military references, uniforms in excess of required amounts, specialized tools. Examples of spouse PBP&E: nursing equipment, legal books, medical reference texts.

📏 Weight Ticket Requirements

  • For PPM/DITY moves, member must obtain certified weight tickets from a certified scale facility
  • Required: one empty-truck weight ticket and one loaded-truck weight ticket
  • Weight tickets must be from a CAT (Certified Automated Terminal) scale or certified public scale
  • Weights must reflect the actual transported goods — personal vehicle weight (if towing) is deducted
  • Document weight tickets on DD Form 3166 (PPM Checklist and Expense Certification)
  • Weight tickets older than 60 days from the actual move date will not be accepted
⚠️If actual shipped weight exceeds authorized allowance, the member pays the excess at the GCC (Government Constructed Cost) rate. Counsel members to weigh before loading to avoid excess charges.
📜 Entitlements by Move Type

Authorized shipments and storage entitlements based on move category per JTR Chapter 5.

🔄 PCS (Permanent Change of Station)

Most common move type. Entitlements are based on rank, dependency status, and origin/destination.

  • HHG Shipment: Up to weight allowance from JTR Table 5-8
  • NTS (Non-Temporary Storage): Up to 1 year CONUS; up to member's tour length OCONUS. Extension via DD Form 3151.
  • TLE (Temporary Lodging Expense): Up to 10 days CONUS; up to 60 days OCONUS (JTR ¶0504)
  • PPM/DITY: Eligible; complete DD Form 3166. Incentive = 95% of GCC
  • POV Shipment: One POV OCONUS only (unless no vehicle allowed at gaining duty station)
  • DPS Required: All shipments must be booked through Defense Personal Property System

🏠 Separation / ETS — HOR & PLEAD

  • Authorized shipment from last duty station to HOR (DD Form 3155) or HOS for 20+ year members (DD Form 3156)
  • Move must be completed within 1 year of separation date (extendable via DD 3151 with good cause)
  • Members with less than 8 years of service may have reduced entitlements — verify with JTR ¶0540

Place Entered Active Duty (PLEAD) — JTR ¶0539

When a member separates or retires and their HOR differs from their current duty station, the government transportation entitlement is to the Home of Record (HOR) — typically the same as the member's PLEAD (Place of Entry on Active Duty).

  • What PLEAD Means: PLEAD is the geographic location where the member entered active military service. For most enlisted members, this is their recruiting station city/state; for officers, it is typically where they were commissioned.
  • HOR vs. PLEAD: In most cases, the HOR and PLEAD are the same location. When they differ (e.g., member moved to a different state before enlisting and changed their official HOR), the HOR on record governs the transportation entitlement — not the PLEAD.
  • Correcting HOR: A member's HOR is established at accession and can only be corrected if there was an error at the time of entry. It cannot be changed simply because the member has lived elsewhere during service. Corrections require documentary evidence and are processed through the Service personnel records office.
  • Government pays to HOR: Upon separation, the government pays transportation costs to the HOR (or HOS if qualifying). If the member elects to move to a location other than HOR/HOS, the government pays only up to the constructive cost to the HOR — the member pays the difference.
  • PLEAD for Civilians: DoD civilians do not have an equivalent PLEAD entitlement. Civilian PCOS transportation is to the new official duty station or to the employee's home at the time of appointment, as specified in their appointment documents.
ℹ️If a member's HOR is in dispute or appears incorrect on their orders, the counselor should direct the member to their S1/G1/MPF/IPAC to resolve the HOR discrepancy before booking the shipment. Shipping to a wrong HOR cannot be corrected after delivery.

🎖️ Retirement — Transportation Extensions (JTR ¶050601)

Retiring members have significant flexibility in when they use their final transportation entitlement. Extensions are governed by JTR ¶050601 and must be formally requested.

Standard Entitlement

  • Entitled to one final shipment to HOR or HOS (DD Form 3155 / DD Form 3156)
  • Move must begin within 1 year of retirement date (standard, no request needed)
  • HOS must be established before retirement date; cannot be changed after shipment is booked

Extension to 3 Years — JTR ¶050601-B

  • A retiring member may request an extension of the transportation entitlement to 3 years from the date of retirement
  • Request must be submitted to the losing installation transportation office before the 1-year standard deadline expires
  • Approval authority: Installation commander or designated transportation official
  • Justification required — common approved reasons: financial hardship, ongoing medical treatment, dependent school year, housing market conditions, or family circumstances
  • Extension is documented in DPS and on DD Form 3151 (Entitlement Extension Request)
  • HHG may remain in NTS during the extension period at government expense (subject to NTS limits)

Extension to 6 Years — JTR ¶050601-C

  • In cases of unusual or compelling circumstances, an extension to 6 years from the date of retirement may be authorized
  • Request must be submitted and approved before the 3-year extension expires
  • Approval authority: Generally requires approval at a higher level than the installation — Service HQ transportation directorate level (e.g., HQ IMCOM G4 for Army, NAVPERSCOM for Navy)
  • Compelling circumstances required — examples: documented medical condition preventing move, dependent in critical education program, housing unavailability at HOS, or ongoing legal proceedings affecting property
  • Member must re-justify the extension; general inconvenience does not qualify for the 6-year extension
  • The 6-year extension is a maximum — no further extensions are authorized beyond 6 years from retirement date
  • NTS continues at government expense during the authorized extension period; member is responsible for any NTS costs beyond the extension expiration

Key Counselor Actions

  • Brief every retiree on the 1-year standard, 3-year extension, and 6-year exception at the retirement counseling appointment
  • Emphasize that extension requests must be submitted before the current deadline expires — retroactive extensions are not authorized
  • Document the extension briefing in DPS counseling notes
  • Provide retiree with POC for extension requests at the losing ITO/PPO
⚠️NTS Cost Warning: While HHG may remain in NTS during an approved extension, NTS fees and conditions must be reviewed annually. If goods are damaged or lost during NTS due to member's delay, FRV claim eligibility may be affected. Counsel retirees to move as soon as practical even if an extension is approved.
ℹ️Surviving Spouses: The surviving spouse of a member who dies on active duty or after retirement retains the transportation entitlement with the same 1-year (extendable to 3 years) timeline measured from the date of the member's death — per JTR ¶050602.

🕊️ Death / Survivor Benefits

  • Surviving family authorized one HHG shipment to HOR or chosen destination
  • HOR transportation authority documented on DD Form 3155
  • Surviving spouse has 3 years from date of death to use entitlement (JTR ¶0544); extendable to 6 years under compelling circumstances per JTR ¶050601-C
  • Coordinate with Casualty Affairs office — do not begin shipment without Casualty travel orders

📦 TDY/TAD (Temporary Duty)

  • No HHG shipment authorized for standard TDY/TAD under 180 days
  • TDY over 180 days: may qualify for HHG shipment (HHG to TDY location)
  • Unaccompanied baggage shipment may be authorized — coordinate with gaining installation
  • NTS authorized to store HHG while member is on extended TDY

💀 Death / Survivor Benefits

  • Surviving family authorized one HHG shipment to HOR or chosen destination
  • HOR transportation authority documented on DD Form 3155
  • Surviving spouse has 3 years from date of death to use entitlement (JTR ¶0544)
  • Coordinate with Casualty Affairs office — do not begin shipment without Casualty travel orders

🌏 OCONUS-Specific Rules

  • POV shipment: one vehicle authorized to/from most OCONUS locations (verify with gaining installation)

✈️ Unaccompanied Baggage (UAB) — Full Reference

UAB is an expedited air freight shipment that travels ahead of or concurrent with the member to provide essential daily items at the OCONUS destination before the main HHG surface shipment arrives (typically 30–90 days later). UAB weight is chargeable against the member's total HHG weight allowance.

What to Ship as UAB

  • Clothing and uniforms sufficient for the first 30–60 days at the new location
  • Children's immediate school supplies, textbooks, and uniforms
  • Small essential kitchen items (coffee maker, basic cookware, utensils)
  • Bedding and linens for immediate use
  • Portable electronics for daily use (laptop, tablet — batteries must comply with airline/air freight rules)
  • Important documents (keep originals; ship certified copies if needed)
  • Small appliances critical to daily routine

UAB Restrictions

  • Firearms — NOT permitted in UAB. Document on DD Form 3152 and include in main HHG only
  • Alcohol — NOT permitted in UAB; must be in main HHG shipment with DD Form 3169
  • Food items — Strongly discouraged in UAB returning OCONUS-to-CONUS due to CBP/USDA inspection; complete DD Form 3158 for main HHG
  • Hazardous materials — Same prohibitions as HHG; aerosols, flammables, propane prohibited
  • Large single items — Items exceeding 300 lbs individually must go in main HHG shipment
  • Lithium batteries over 100Wh — Air freight restrictions are stricter than truck; verify with TSP/carrier before including battery devices in UAB

Booking UAB in DPS

  • UAB is booked as a separate shipment from the main HHG in DPS
  • Member selects "Unaccompanied Baggage" as the shipment type when setting up the move
  • UAB typically moves via approved air freight carriers under the HHG TSP contract
  • Estimated transit time: 10–21 days to most OCONUS locations (varies by destination)

🎓 PLEAD — Pre-move Dependent Travel to HOR

PLEAD authorizes a member's dependents — and a partial HHG shipment for their use — to travel to the member's Home of Record (HOR) in advance of the member's PCS move, separation, or retirement. It is commonly used to place school-age children in a stable school environment or to reunite the family with support networks before the member's tour is complete.

PLEAD Eligibility (JTR ¶0549)

  • Member must hold approved PCS, retirement, or separation orders, or be within 12 months of anticipated retirement/separation
  • Dependents must be command-sponsored and authorized to travel under the member's orders
  • Receiving location must be the member's established HOR as recorded in the official personnel system
  • Member must not have previously used the full HHG shipment entitlement for this PCS/retirement move
  • PLEAD must be approved by the ITO/PPO and documented in DPS before dependents depart

What PLEAD Authorizes

  • Transportation (travel) of dependents to the HOR at dependent travel rates per JTR
  • A partial HHG shipment to the HOR for the dependents' use — counted against the member's total weight allowance
  • Member retains the ability to ship remaining HHG (within weight limit) when they complete their tour
  • NTS for HHG not shipped under PLEAD, pending the member's final move

PLEAD and HOR — Critical Relationship

  • The PLEAD destination must be the member's HOR as recorded in official personnel records — not a preferred location
  • If the member's HOR is incorrect, it must be corrected in the personnel system (DEERS/iPERMS/MCTFS) before the PLEAD shipment is authorized
  • Members retiring with an HOS (DD Form 3156) different from their HOR: the PLEAD shipment to HOR and the final retirement shipment to HOS are separate legs — coordinate carefully with ITO/PPO to avoid exceeding weight allowance across both legs
  • PLEAD uses the HOR authority (DD Form 3155) as the foundational document
⚠️HOR Verification Is Mandatory: Confirm the member's HOR is correctly recorded before authorizing any PLEAD action. An incorrect HOR cannot be corrected retroactively after the shipment departs. This is a counselor verification responsibility.

Counselor Checklist for PLEAD

  • Verify HOR in official personnel system matches the intended destination
  • Confirm orders are valid and dependents are authorized to travel
  • Calculate partial HHG weight and verify it does not exceed total allowance
  • Book PLEAD shipment in DPS as a separate shipment linked to member's orders
  • Authorize NTS for remaining HHG if member will not ship final HHG until tour completion
  • Brief member on HOR-vs-HOS distinction if retirement is anticipated

🎒 Student Travel

Student travel is an annual transportation entitlement that allows a member's college-age dependent child to travel between the member's OCONUS duty station (or the HOR where the student resides) and the student's school in CONUS, Alaska, or Hawaii.

Eligibility Requirements (JTR ¶0551)

  • Member must be assigned to an OCONUS permanent duty station
  • Dependent must be the member's child (stepchild or legally adopted child also qualifies)
  • Dependent must be under age 23 and enrolled full-time at an accredited college, university, or secondary boarding school in CONUS, Alaska, or Hawaii
  • The school must not be accessible from the OCONUS PDS (i.e., the student cannot attend school at the OCONUS location)
  • Authorized once per academic year — round trip

What Student Travel Covers

  • Round-trip commercial air travel at the government rate (lowest available fare consistent with mission requirements)
  • Reasonable checked baggage fees for academic-year supplies
  • Travel is typically routed through the member's OCONUS PDS city or the HOR (wherever the student's authorized residence is)
  • Per diem is not authorized — transportation only

Student Travel Does NOT Include

  • A separate HHG or UAB shipment for the student's personal belongings — these count against the member's HHG allowance if shipped
  • Travel for academic breaks shorter than the full academic year (mid-semester breaks, holidays) — those require personal funds or leave travel
  • Travel for students enrolled at OCONUS institutions or online-only programs where in-person attendance is not required
  • Travel for dependents over age 23, or for spouses

📦 What Is Unaccompanied Baggage (UAB)?

Unaccompanied Baggage (UAB) is a small, expedited shipment transported by air that arrives at the OCONUS duty station ahead of the main HHG surface shipment. It is intended to provide essential household items during the period between arrival and delivery of the main HHG shipment.

  • UAB is authorized for OCONUS PCS moves only — not for CONUS-to-CONUS moves
  • UAB travels by air freight and typically arrives within 2–4 weeks of departure
  • Main HHG surface shipment may take 30–90 days depending on the OCONUS location
  • UAB weight counts against the member's total HHG weight allowance (not in addition to it)
  • UAB is booked separately in DPS from the main HHG shipment
  • Items typically included in UAB: bedding/linens, kitchen essentials, children's school supplies, portable electronics, clothing for the first weeks, and children's comfort items

👨‍👩‍👧 Dependent UAB — Key Rules

  • Each authorized dependent (spouse, children, parents authorized as dependents) is entitled to 350 lbs of UAB when accompanying the member OCONUS
  • Infant/minor children: Each child listed on the member's military ID/orders as a dependent counts separately — a family with 2 children has 4 × 350 = 1,400 lbs of UAB authorized (all chargeable against HHG allowance)
  • Dependents not traveling with member: If dependents are deferred and will join the member later, they retain their UAB entitlement at the time they travel OCONUS. A separate UAB shipment must be booked in DPS.
  • Dependents remaining in CONUS: If dependents will remain in CONUS for the entire tour (split family), dependents do not have a UAB entitlement for the member's OCONUS shipment.
  • New dependents added during OCONUS tour: A member who marries or has a child while OCONUS may request a small UAB shipment for the new dependent — verify with installation PPO/ITO.

🌍 OCONUS-Specific Shipment Guidance

Japan (Atsugi, Yokosuka, Sasebo, Misawa, Okinawa, Iwakuni)

  • Japanese housing is generally smaller than US housing — counsel members to ship essential items only and place excess in NTS CONUS
  • Tatami rooms and sliding door (shoji) openings may not accommodate large furniture
  • UAB typically arrives in 2–3 weeks; surface HHG takes 60–90 days
  • Firearms: Japan SOFA prohibits private ownership off-base. Store CONUS in NTS (see Prohibited Items tab)
  • Food items returning CONUS: DD Form 3158 required

Korea (Camp Humphreys, Yongsan, Osan, Kunsan)

  • Most Korea tours are 12-month unaccompanied (Area I) or 24-month accompanied (Area II/III)
  • Unaccompanied tours: member ships 350 lbs UAB; remaining HHG to NTS

🎓 Student Travel

Transportation entitlements for dependent students of service members and DoD civilians assigned OCONUS. Per JTR ¶058001–058006.

📚 What Is Student Travel?

Student travel is a transportation entitlement that allows dependent children of service members stationed OCONUS to travel to and from an educational institution in the United States (or another authorized location) at government expense. It is separate from PCS move entitlements.

  • Authorized under JTR ¶058001 for members assigned OCONUS with dependent students
  • Provides round-trip transportation between the OCONUS duty station and the school location
  • Intended to allow dependent children to attend US schools, colleges, or vocational institutions when local schooling is unavailable or inadequate
  • Student travel is a travel entitlement, not a personal property entitlement — it covers transportation costs, not HHG or UAB shipment

✅ Eligibility Requirements (JTR ¶058001)

  • Member must be assigned OCONUS on a PCS tour with authorized dependents
  • Student must be a dependent listed on the member's military ID/orders
  • Age requirements: Student must be at least 18 years old (or high school graduate) to qualify for independent student travel. Younger dependent students may travel with or to a parent/guardian.
  • Enrollment requirement: Student must be enrolled or accepted at a qualifying educational institution (university, college, junior college, vocational/technical school, or secondary school)
  • Student is a dependent: The student must still meet the definition of a dependent under the member's service regulations (generally under age 23 if enrolled in school full-time)
  • Accompanied tour: Member must be on an accompanied OCONUS tour (dependents authorized at the duty station)
⚠️Student travel is not authorized for members on unaccompanied tours. If dependents are not authorized at the OCONUS duty station, student travel does not apply — the dependent is not present OCONUS to travel from.

🗺️ Authorized Destinations & Frequency (JTR ¶058002–058003)

Where Student Travel Goes

  • Primary destination: The location of the student's educational institution within the United States (or its territories)
  • Alternate destination: May be authorized to the member's HOR if that is closer to the school, at no additional cost to the government
  • The government pays the cost of transportation not to exceed the cost of travel between the OCONUS duty station and the student's school (or HOR, whichever is less)

Frequency of Travel

  • Student travel is authorized once each academic year (round trip) per qualifying dependent student
  • Travel should coincide with school year breaks — typically summer between academic years or semester breaks
  • Member may authorize the student to travel to the HOR instead of returning to the OCONUS duty station if that better supports the family situation

One-Time Limitation

  • Student travel for a given dependent is limited to the duration of the member's current OCONUS tour
  • Once the member PCS's back to CONUS, student travel entitlement ends
  • On a new OCONUS PCS tour, student travel may begin again if the dependent still qualifies

💰 What Student Travel Covers (JTR ¶058004)

  • Transportation cost: Government-procured ticket (typically commercial air) from the OCONUS duty station to the school/HOR and return
  • Per diem: Student is entitled to per diem at the dependent rate for days of travel (travel days only — not for the academic year while at school)
  • Baggage: Normal airline baggage allowance; no separate HHG or UAB shipment is authorized for student travel
  • Student travel does not cover: room and board at school, tuition, school supplies, local transportation at school, or personal baggage beyond normal airline limits
ℹ️Student travel is coordinated through the installation finance/travel office, not the transportation/personal property office. However, counselors should be aware of the entitlement to properly advise members and refer them to the correct office.

🏫 Qualifying Educational Institutions

  • Accredited US colleges and universities (4-year, community college, graduate programs)
  • Accredited vocational, technical, or trade schools
  • Accredited secondary schools (high schools) — when local schooling is unavailable or the student has special educational needs
  • DoDEA (Department of Defense Education Activity) schools in CONUS or other OCONUS locations may qualify in some circumstances — verify with installation education office
  • Online-only programs: Generally do not qualify unless the student has a physical campus enrollment requirement

⬛ Army — AR 55-71

The Army transportation regulation implements JTR within Army-specific procedures and command structures.

Key Army-Specific Rules

  • Installation Transportation Office (ITO): All Army PCS moves must be coordinated through the local ITO before departure. Members cannot bypass ITO and book directly in DPS without ITO authorization code.
  • Pre-Move Survey: Army strongly recommends an in-home pre-move survey for moves over 10,000 lbs. ITO will schedule with TSP if requested.
  • Excess Weight: Army commanders can authorize one-time weight allowance waivers for field-grade officers on certain OCONUS assignments — requires G4 coordination.
  • OCONUS Korea (USAG Korea): Restricted dependent area — limited HHG authorized. Check Area I vs. Area II assignments. Unaccompanied members: NTS authorized for remainder of tour.
  • OCONUS Germany/Italy: Full accompanied tour = full HHG allowance. SOFA permits firearms shipment with host nation customs declaration. DD Form 3152 required.
  • DITY/PPM: Army members may be authorized advance pay for PPM expenses — coordinate with finance before move. Document on DD Form 3166.
  • POV Storage: Available at Army Vehicle Processing Centers (VPCs). DD Forms 3163/3164 required. Major VPCs: Baltimore, Houston, Jacksonville, Los Angeles.
  • Unaccompanied Baggage: Army authorizes up to 1,000 lbs unaccompanied baggage for OCONUS PCS when full HHG shipment is delayed.
📞Army HQ Contact: HQ IMCOM G4 Transportation Division — coordinates Army-wide personal property policy. Local ITO is primary POC for all individual move issues.

⚓ Navy — MILPERSMAN 4650-010

Navy personal property procedures are governed by MILPERSMAN 4650-010 and NAVSUP Publication 490. Navy-unique shipment rules apply to unaccompanied tours, ship deployments, and shore rotations.

Key Navy-Specific Rules

  • PPO (Personal Property Office): Navy members counsel at the local PPO (Navy Installations Command). Some large installations have dedicated HHG counseling centers (e.g., Norfolk, San Diego, Pearl Harbor).
  • Deployment NTS: Sailors deploying 6+ months may store HHG in NTS rather than paying rent on an apartment. NTS is authorized for the duration of deployment plus 30 days (extendable via DD 3151).
  • Ship-to-Shore PCS: When moving from a ship to shore duty, members have 60 days to complete counseling after check-in at new command.
  • Japan (OCONUS): Yokosuka, Sasebo, and Misawa have restricted weight limits due to housing dimensions. Counselors must brief members on Japanese housing square footage limitations. Excess stored in NTS at member's expense beyond JTR NTS limit.
  • Guam: Full HHG authorized. Shipping time is 60–90 days by sea. Counsel members to ship early and plan for temporary housing/furniture rental.
  • Firearms in Japan: SOFA Article 9 prohibits private firearm possession in Japan for US personnel. DD Form 3152 must note "destination prohibits — storing CONUS." NTS or surrender required.
  • Unaccompanied Baggage (UAB): Navy authorizes up to 600 lbs UAB for OCONUS moves. Arrives before main shipment by air.
  • POV: Navy VPCs operated at major ports. Coordinate through NAVSUP for overseas POV shipment timelines.
⚠️Navy Instruction OPNAVINST 4600.15 governs special move categories including embassy duty, recruiting duty, and overseas recruiting tours — verify with BUPERS for these assignment types.

🦅 Marine Corps — MCO 4600.40

Marine Corps personal property is governed by MCO 4600.40 and coordinated through installation PPOs at each Marine Corps installation.

Key USMC-Specific Rules

  • PPO Locations: USMC PPOs at Camp Lejeune, Camp Pendleton, Quantico, Okinawa, Iwakuni, and Miramar. Members at smaller installations may use nearest major PPO or coordinate through MCCS online portal.
  • Okinawa: Most Okinawa tours are accompanied (36 months) or unaccompanied (12 months). Accompanied: full HHG authorized with housing size caveat. Unaccompanied: HHG placed in NTS; member ships only UAB (300 lbs max).
  • Unaccompanied Baggage: USMC authorizes 300 lbs UAB for OCONUS moves (less than Navy's 600 lbs). Verify current MCO for any recent changes.
  • Mandatory Pre-Move Survey: MCO 4600.40 requires a pre-move survey for all shipments over 12,000 lbs or when member has art, antiques, or high-value items.
  • PPM/DITY: Marines processing a DITY move must have orders certified and DD Form 3166 completed by PPO before move begins. Advance pay authorized up to 60% of estimated incentive.
  • Retirement HOS: Marine retirees completing DD Form 3156 must have commanding officer endorsement if HOS is more than 500 miles outside CONUS area of military service.
  • Weapons: USMC permits shipment of authorized firearms per DD Form 3152. Bayonets, suppressors, and NFA items require USMC legal review prior to shipment authorization.
  • MARADMIN Guidance: Check current MARADMIN for temporary policy changes affecting PCS moves — issued frequently during high ops-tempo periods.

⚓ Coast Guard — COMDTINST M4050.6

The Coast Guard follows JTR entitlements but has unique administrative processes through CGPSC (Coast Guard Personnel Service Center) and SWA (Surface Warfare Afloat) commands.

Key USCG-Specific Rules

  • CGPSC Coordination: All personal property moves are coordinated through CGPSC (formerly CGPC). Members contact their servicing Personnel and Pay Center for move authorization codes.
  • Remote Duty Stations: USCG has numerous remote/isolated duty stations (light stations, LORAN sites, Alaska). For remote assignments with no road access, special air or water freight shipment may be authorized — coordinate with CG-731.
  • Alaska: Extended shipping times (60–90 days surface freight). Air freight for urgent items authorized up to 350 lbs. Counsel members to ship well in advance.
  • Hawaii/Guam: Similar to other OCONUS locations. USCG has limited housing inventory — members should apply for housing immediately upon receipt of orders.
  • POV: USCG uses commercial VPCs under GSA contract. No dedicated USCG VPC; members coordinate through CGPSC for commercial POV shipper authorization.
  • Small Units: Many USCG members are at small units (Aids to Navigation Teams, Sectors) without on-site counselors. CGPSC provides phone/virtual counseling support. Refer members to 1-855-CG-MOVES.
  • USCG Reserve: Reserve members called to active duty for 30+ days in support of contingency operations may be entitled to HHG shipment — verify with CGPSC and orders type.
  • DITY/PPM: USCG members completing PPM must submit DD Form 3166 and weight tickets to CGPSC within 45 days of move completion for incentive payment.
ℹ️USCG members may use any DoD PPO for in-person counseling. CGPSC provides authorization codes. Navy and USCG have a memorandum of understanding for mutual PPO use at shared installations.

🚫 Prohibited & Restricted Items

Items that cannot be shipped in government-arranged HHG. Brief members thoroughly — improper shipment can result in criminal liability.

🚨Criminal Liability: Shipping prohibited items (especially hazardous materials or illegal weapons) can result in UCMJ action, federal prosecution, and/or termination of civilian employment. Counselor must document the prohibited items briefing.

🚫 Absolutely Prohibited — Cannot Ship Under Any Circumstances

Hazardous / Flammable / Explosive Materials

  • Ammunition of any type (including empty brass casings with primers)
  • Black powder, gun powder, or explosive compounds
  • Fireworks, flares, or pyrotechnics
  • Propane tanks, butane, or any pressurized fuel containers (even empty)
  • Gasoline, diesel, kerosene — including items with residual fuel (mowers, generators)
  • Paint thinner, acetone, lacquer, turpentine
  • Aerosol cans (hairspray, bug spray, etc.)

Illegal / Controlled Substances

  • Marijuana and cannabis products (illegal under federal law regardless of state law)
  • Schedule I–V controlled substances without valid prescription
  • Drug paraphernalia

Biological / Perishable Hazards

  • Live plants, seeds, and soil (without USDA import permit)
  • Fresh food, meat, produce, or refrigerated items
  • Live animals or insects
  • Human or animal remains

Weapons of Mass Destruction / Illegal Weapons

  • Unregistered NFA weapons (suppressed items, short-barreled rifles without ATF approval)
  • Destructive devices, grenades, or military ordinance
  • Switchblades or other weapons prohibited in destination state/country

🔒 Gun Safes — Special Handling Guidance

Gun safes are authorized for shipment in government-arranged HHG but require specific counseling due to their weight, value, and contents.

Weight Considerations

  • Residential gun safes typically weigh 200–1,000+ lbs and count toward the member's HHG weight allowance
  • Large safes (500+ lbs) may require a TSP with heavy-item capability — advise member to disclose safe weight when booking in DPS
  • TSPs may charge additional labor fees for safes requiring special equipment (dollies, ramps, extra crew) — this is within TSP contract terms

Contents Requirements — CRITICAL

  • Firearms must be removed from the safe before pickup and shipped per DD Form 3152 procedures
  • Ammunition is prohibited — must not be left inside the safe under any circumstances
  • Valuables (jewelry, cash, documents) should be removed and hand-carried
  • Safe must be empty and unlocked (or key/combination provided to TSP) for inspection at delivery

FRV Coverage

  • List the gun safe on DD Form 3153 with brand, model, and estimated replacement value
  • Photograph all sides before pickup to document pre-existing condition
  • Note any exterior damage at delivery before signing the inventory
⚠️If a gun safe arrives with ammunition still inside, the TSP is required to report to local law enforcement. Counsel members explicitly: remove all firearms AND all ammunition before pickup day.

♿ Medical Equipment & Durable Medical Equipment (DME)

Members and civilians with medical conditions, or dependents requiring medical equipment, have specific entitlements and handling requirements for personal property moves.

Authorized DME for Shipment

  • Power wheelchairs and motorized scooters — authorized; batteries must comply with lithium battery rules (see below)
  • Manual wheelchairs, walkers, crutches — authorized; no special handling required
  • CPAP / BiPAP machines — authorized; strongly recommend hand-carrying to avoid claims issues
  • Hospital beds, patient lifts, traction equipment — authorized; provide advance notice in DPS for TSP capacity planning
  • Nebulizers, infusion pumps, dialysis equipment — authorized; document on inventory for FRV purposes via DD Form 3153
  • Hearing aids and cochlear implant processors — authorized; high value; recommend hand-carry and list on DD Form 3153

Prohibited Medical Items

  • Pressurized oxygen tanks (full or empty) — PROHIBITED. Classified as compressed gas. Member must arrange direct delivery from oxygen supplier at destination. Coordinate with TRICARE and medical provider well in advance of move date.
  • Liquid nitrogen or dry ice containers — PROHIBITED. Cryogenic/sublimating materials are hazardous in enclosed transport vehicles.
  • Controlled substance medications — PROHIBITED in HHG. Must be hand-carried in original labeled containers.

Prescription Medications

  • All medications should be hand-carried — never packed in HHG boxes
  • Controlled substances (Schedule II–V) must travel with the member; packing in HHG may violate DEA regulations
  • OCONUS moves: research destination country import requirements. Some US medications (certain stimulants, strong opioids) are illegal in Japan, UAE, and other SOFA nations — counsel member to carry prescriptions and a provider letter
ℹ️EFMP Coordination: Members enrolled in the Exceptional Family Member Program (EFMP) with dependents requiring ongoing DME should coordinate with the installation EFMP office before booking. Gaining installation EFMP enrollment and DME provider continuity must be confirmed before departure.

🚛 PPM / DITY Moves

Personally Procured Move (PPM) formerly called Do-It-Yourself (DITY). Governed by JTR ¶0523 and DTR Part IV. Primary form: DD Form 3166.

💰 PPM Overview & Incentive Payment

The PPM program allows members to personally arrange their own move in exchange for 95% of the Government Constructive Cost (GCC) — the estimated cost the government would have paid a TSP to perform the same move.

  • Incentive = 95% × GCC (based on weight shipped and distance)
  • Weight shipped must be verified by certified weight tickets (empty and loaded)
  • Partial PPM is permitted (member moves some goods; government TSP moves remainder)
  • PPM and government shipment combined cannot exceed authorized weight allowance
  • Incentive payment is taxable income (reported on W-2)
ℹ️Members who exceed their authorized weight limit on a PPM are paid at 95% of GCC only for the authorized weight — not the full shipped weight. Counsel members to weigh before loading.

📋 DD Form 3166 — PPM Checklist and Expense Certification

DD Form 3166 is the central document for all PPM moves. It replaced DD Form 2278 effective January 2026. Sections include:

  • Section I: Member identification and order information
  • Section II: PPM authorization and GCC calculation
  • Section III: Weight ticket documentation (empty/loaded, date, scale facility)
  • Section IV: Expense documentation (rental truck, packing materials, fuel, tolls)
  • Section V: Member certification and signature
  • Section VI: Finance/ITO approval and incentive payment calculation
⚠️DD Form 3166 must be completed and approved BEFORE the member begins the PPM move. Retroactive PPM approval is not authorized. Unauthorized PPM moves will not receive incentive payment.

📝 PPM Process — Step by Step

  • Step 1: Member receives PCS orders and reports to ITO/PPO within 7 days
  • Step 2: Member completes DD Form 3162 (Pre-Counseling Worksheet) prior to appointment
  • Step 3: Counselor calculates GCC estimate and completes DD Form 3166 Sections I–II with member
  • Step 4: Counselor briefs prohibited items, weight requirements, and weight ticket procedures
  • Step 5: ITO approves DD Form 3166. Advance pay may be requested (up to 60% of estimated incentive)
  • Step 6: Member obtains empty weight ticket before loading
  • Step 7: Member loads and obtains loaded weight ticket
  • Step 8: Member completes move to destination
  • Step 9: Member submits DD Form 3166 (completed), weight tickets, receipts, and DD Form 1351-2 to finance
  • Step 10: Finance calculates final incentive payment and disburses

⚠️ Common PPM Errors — Counselor Action Required

  • Missing empty weight ticket: PPM cannot be certified without both empty and loaded weights. Brief member repeatedly.
  • Scale not certified: Only CAT scales or certified commercial scales are acceptable. Bathroom scales, post office scales, and ag scales are not valid.
  • Moved before approval: Without approved DD 3166, no payment is authorized. Flag these cases immediately to supervisor.
  • Weight over allowance: Calculate before approving. Member is responsible for any weight over their authorized allowance — they will not receive incentive for excess pounds.
  • Combining PPM and government shipment improperly: Both shipments combined must not exceed authorized weight. DPS tracking is essential.
🚗 POV Shipment & Storage

Privately Owned Vehicle (POV) shipping and storage entitlements under JTR ¶0537. Forms: DD 3163, DD 3164, DD 3165.

🌏 OCONUS POV Shipment — DD Form 3163

Members authorized to ship one POV to/from OCONUS duty stations during PCS. DD Form 3163 (POV Shipping Counseling Form) must be completed prior to vehicle drop-off at VPC.

  • One POV authorized per member (regardless of number of vehicles owned)
  • Vehicle must be a personal vehicle titled in member's name (or spouse/dependent)
  • Vehicle must be operable and meet customs requirements of destination country
  • Member responsible for ensuring vehicle meets SOFA and host nation standards (emissions, size, modifications)
  • Shipping time: 30–60 days average; up to 90 days for Pacific locations
  • Vehicle Processing Centers (VPC): Member drops off and picks up POV. Inspect vehicle thoroughly at both drop-off and pick-up; note any pre-existing damage on DD Form 3163.
⚠️Germany and some EU countries may require vehicle emissions modifications (catalytic converter upgrades, etc.) before allowing vehicle registration. Counsel members to research destination country requirements before shipping.

🏭 POV Storage — DD Forms 3164 & 3165

When a member is assigned to a location where POV use is not practical (deployments, unaccompanied OCONUS tours, etc.), POV storage is authorized.

  • DD Form 3164: POV Storage Counseling Form — documents vehicle info, storage authorization, duration, and member's chosen storage facility
  • DD Form 3165: POV Storage Authorization Letter — issued by installation transportation officer confirming assignment qualifies for POV storage
  • Government-funded storage is limited to one POV per member
  • Storage duration = length of the assignment/deployment that qualifies + 30 days for retrieval
  • Member may store at government VPC or approved commercial facility
  • Member must retrieve or make arrangements within 30 days of return or storage fees become member's responsibility

📋 Claims & Full Replacement Value

FRV coverage briefing requirements and claims process. Primary form: DD Form 3153.

🛡️ Full Replacement Value (FRV) — DD Form 3153

FRV is the primary cargo loss/damage coverage for government-arranged HHG shipments. The Transportation Service Provider (TSP) is responsible for FRV coverage under their contract.

  • FRV = replacement cost of item at today's price (not depreciated value)
  • No additional cost to the member for standard FRV coverage
  • Coverage applies to loss or damage that occurs while goods are in TSP's custody
  • Members may elect Released Value at $0.60/lb (lower coverage, not recommended)
  • High-value items (over $5,000 individual item) should be listed on DD Form 3153 with description and estimated value
⚠️DD Form 3153 must be completed at the counseling appointment — not at delivery. Once the shipment departs, coverage elections cannot be changed. Counsel members on all options before signing.

📋 Claims Counseling Requirements

Every member must receive FRV counseling and complete DD Form 3153 before shipment:

  • Explain difference between FRV and Released Value ($0.60/lb)
  • Advise member to document high-value items with photos and serial numbers before packing
  • Remind member that prohibited items are not covered if they cause damage
  • Brief the 180-day filing window and how to access DPS claims module
  • Provide TSP contact information at time of shipment booking
  • Advise member to be present at delivery and inspect all items before signing the delivery receipt

💡 Counselor Tips for Claims Prevention

  • Encourage members to photograph all high-value items before packing begins
  • Advise against packing irreplaceable items (jewelry, important documents, heirlooms) in HHG — suggest hand-carry
  • Remind members that electronics serial numbers should be recorded on DD Form 3153 for FRV purposes
  • Items packed by owner (PBO) have limited TSP liability for internal damage — advise professional packing for fragile items
  • Firearms must be secured in TSA-approved hard-sided cases; document on DD Form 3152 and FRV listing

📋 What Is RITA?

The Relocation Income Tax Allowance (RITA) is a payment designed to reimburse service members and DoD civilians for the additional federal income tax burden incurred as a result of receiving taxable PCS/PCOS moving allowances. It specifically addresses the "tax on a tax" problem created by the Withholding Tax Allowance (WTA).

The Tax Problem RITA Solves

  • Certain PCS entitlements (such as moving expense reimbursements and allowances) are taxable income under the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017
  • When these reimbursements are paid, the government also pays a Withholding Tax Allowance (WTA) to cover the estimated federal income tax owed
  • However, the WTA itself is taxable income — creating a "tax on a tax" cycle
  • RITA reimburses the member for the additional federal income tax they owe on the WTA, effectively making the member whole and neutralizing the tax burden of the PCS/PCOS move
ℹ️RITA does not cover state income taxes. Members in high-tax states (CA, NY, etc.) may still owe state tax on WTA income. Counsel members to consult a tax professional or MilTax (free military tax service) for their full tax picture.

📝 Taxable PCS Entitlements (Require WTA/RITA)

Since the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017, most PCS moving expense reimbursements are taxable for most service members. Common taxable items include:

  • PPM/DITY Incentive Payment — the 95% of GCC incentive is taxable income; WTA is applied at the time of payment
  • Miscellaneous Expense Allowance (MEA) — lump sum paid to offset incidental PCS costs; taxable
  • Temporary Lodging Expense (TLE) reimbursements — taxable under current IRS rules
  • Real Estate Transaction Expenses (CONUS) — home sale/purchase reimbursements for certain categories are taxable
⚠️Exception — Combat Zone Members: Service members whose PCS move is in connection with a permanent change of station while serving in a combat zone may have certain tax exemptions. Refer to IRS Publication 3 (Armed Forces Tax Guide) and MilTax for current guidance.

📅 RITA Filing Process

  • Step 1 — Complete Tax Return: In the tax year following the WTA payment, member files their federal tax return (or completes a draft) to determine actual tax liability
  • Step 2 — Obtain W-2 and Move Documents: Member gathers W-2 showing WTA income, travel vouchers, and PCS-related finance documents
  • Step 3 — File RITA Claim: Member submits RITA claim through the Defense Finance and Accounting Service (DFAS) MyPay portal or through the installation finance office
  • Step 4 — Finance Calculates RITA Amount: DFAS calculates the additional federal income tax attributable to the WTA income using IRS tax tables
  • Step 5 — RITA Payment: DFAS pays the RITA amount to the member, completing the tax reimbursement cycle
✅MilTax: DoD offers free tax preparation assistance through MilTax (available via Military OneSource). Counsel members to use MilTax for RITA-related tax preparation — the preparers are trained on military-specific tax issues including WTA and RITA.

💼 RITA for DoD Civilians (PCOS)

DoD civilian employees directed to perform a PCOS are also eligible for WTA and RITA on taxable relocation reimbursements. Key differences from military:

  • Civilian RITA is processed through the employing agency's finance/HR office, not DFAS
  • Civilian taxable relocation expenses include: PPM incentive, certain travel reimbursements, and house-hunting trip expenses
  • Civilian employees should coordinate with their agency's relocation coordinator for RITA filing procedures
  • Some civilian agencies use third-party relocation management companies (RMCs) — RITA filing may go through the RMC portal
  • Civilian RITA timeline: file in the tax year following WTA receipt, same as military

💡 Counselor Responsibilities — RITA Briefing

While RITA is primarily a finance function, personal property counselors play an important role in ensuring members understand the tax implications of their move:

  • Brief every member receiving a PPM incentive that the payment is taxable income and WTA will be applied
  • Inform members that they will need to file a RITA claim in the following tax year to complete the reimbursement cycle
  • Direct members to their installation finance office for WTA/RITA details and to Military OneSource for MilTax assistance
  • Document RITA briefing on DD Form 3166 (PPM) or in DPS counseling notes
  • Do not attempt to calculate RITA amounts — this is a finance function requiring current IRS tax tables

📖 Glossary

Key terms and abbreviations used in the DoD personal property program.

Durable Medical Equipment (DME)
Medically necessary equipment prescribed for home use: power wheelchairs, hospital beds, CPAP machines, patient lifts, etc. Authorized for shipment in HHG with special handling considerations for lithium-battery-powered units. Pressurized oxygen tanks are prohibited.
Exceptional Family Member Program (EFMP)
DoD program for service members with family members who have special medical or educational needs. EFMP enrollment is mandatory and must be verified at both losing and gaining installations before PCS moves involving dependents with special needs or DME requirements.
The online portal used by service members, DoD civilians, and transportation offices to book, manage, and track all government-arranged personal property shipments. All PCS shipments must be booked through DPS.
Defense Transportation Regulation (DTR)
Multi-part DoD regulation governing transportation operations. Part IV covers personal property. The January 2026 edition introduced the DD Form 3151–3178 series.
Full Replacement Value (FRV)
Cargo loss/damage coverage under government HHG shipment contracts. TSP pays the current retail replacement cost of lost or damaged items — not depreciated value. Counseled via DD Form 3153. Member has 180 days from delivery to file.
Government Constructive Cost (GCC)
The estimated cost the government would have paid a TSP to perform a given move. Used to calculate PPM incentive payments (95% of GCC).
Household Goods (HHG)
Personal property belonging to a member and their dependents that is authorized for shipment at government expense during a PCS move.
Home of Record (HOR)
The place listed on the member's enlistment/appointment documents as their home at the time of entry into military service. Members separating or retiring may ship HHG to HOR via DD Form 3155.
Home of Selection (HOS)
A location chosen by a member retiring with 20+ years of service that may differ from their HOR. Authorized via DD Form 3156. Transportation time limit is 1 year from retirement date, extendable to 3 years (standard) or 6 years (exceptional) per JTR ¶054202.
PLEAD
Pre-move authorization allowing a member's dependents and a partial HHG shipment to travel to the member's Home of Record (HOR) in advance of the member's PCS, retirement, or separation. Requires valid orders, established HOR, and ITO/PPO approval in DPS before departure. Governed by JTR ¶0549. Partial HHG weight counts against total allowance.
Student Travel
An annual transportation entitlement (JTR ¶0551) authorizing round-trip travel for a member's full-time enrolled college-age dependent (under 23) between their CONUS/AK/HI school and the member's OCONUS permanent duty station or HOR. Transportation only — no per diem, no HHG shipment.
Unaccompanied Baggage (UAB)
An expedited air freight shipment authorized for OCONUS PCS moves. Arrives before main HHG surface shipment (estimated 10–21 days). Weight limits: Army/AF 1,000 lbs; Navy 600 lbs (+200 with dependents); USMC 300 lbs; USCG 350 lbs. Weight is chargeable against total HHG allowance. Firearms and alcohol are not permitted in UAB.
Installation Transportation Office (ITO)
The installation-level office responsible for personal property counseling, DPS authorization codes, and transportation coordination. Army-specific term; Navy/USMC/USCG use PPO.
Joint Travel Regulations (JTR)
The primary DoD regulation governing all travel and transportation entitlements for Uniformed Service members and DoD civilians. Chapter 5 covers household goods and personal property.
Non-Temporary Storage (NTS)
Long-term storage of HHG at government expense, authorized when a member is assigned to a location where HHG cannot be used (OCONUS unaccompanied tours, deployments, etc.). Limited to 1 year CONUS; tour length OCONUS. Extension via DD Form 3151.
Permanent Change of Official Station (PCOS)
The civilian equivalent of a military PCS — a directed, permanent relocation of a DoD civilian employee to a new duty location. Triggers personal property entitlements under JTR Chapter 5 Part C.
Permanent Change of Station (PCS)
A military order directing a service member to permanently relocate from one duty station to another. Triggers full HHG, NTS, and related personal property entitlements.
Personally Procured Move / Do-It-Yourself (PPM/DITY)
An authorized move type in which the member personally arranges their own transportation in exchange for 95% of the GCC. Documented and authorized on DD Form 3166.
Personal Property Office (PPO)
Navy, USMC, and USCG term for the installation-level personal property counseling office. Performs the same function as the Army ITO.
Professional Books, Papers, and Equipment (PBP&E)
Job-related professional items authorized for shipment in addition to (non-chargeable against) the standard HHG weight allowance. Member: up to 2,000 lbs; spouse: up to 500 lbs. Documented on DD Form 3168.
Privately Owned Vehicle (POV)
A vehicle owned by a member or dependent. One POV may be shipped at government expense for OCONUS PCS moves. Documented on DD Form 3163 (shipment) or DD Forms 3164/3165 (storage).
Status of Forces Agreement (SOFA)
A bilateral agreement between the US and a host nation governing the legal status and rights of US military personnel stationed in that country. SOFA provisions affect firearms permissions, vehicle registration, and personal property restrictions for OCONUS moves.
Transportation Service Provider (TSP)
A commercial moving company contracted by the government to perform HHG moves. TSPs are selected through DPS and are responsible for FRV coverage, inventory, and on-time delivery performance.
Place Entered Active Duty (PLEAD)
The geographic location where a service member entered active military service (e.g., recruiting station city for enlisted, commissioning source location for officers). Closely related to HOR. When separating or retiring, government transportation is authorized to the HOR — which in most cases aligns with the PLEAD. Discrepancies must be corrected through the personnel records office before booking a separation/retirement shipment.
Student Travel (JTR ¶058001)
An annual round-trip transportation entitlement allowing dependent students of OCONUS-assigned service members to travel to/from their US educational institution at government expense. Authorized once per academic year per qualifying dependent. Processed through the finance/travel office (DTS), not the personal property office. Does not include HHG or UAB — normal baggage only.
Retirement Transportation Extension (JTR ¶050601)
Provision allowing retiring members to extend the 1-year transportation entitlement deadline: to 3 years with approved justification (financial hardship, medical, family circumstances), and to 6 years under compelling circumstances with higher-level approval. Requests must be submitted before the current deadline expires. Maximum extension is 6 years from retirement date — no further extensions authorized.

Withholding Tax Allowance (WTA)
An advance payment made by the government to cover the estimated federal income tax owed on taxable PCS/PCOS moving allowances (such as PPM incentive payments). Paid in the same pay period as the taxable allowance. The WTA itself is taxable income, which is why RITA exists to complete the reimbursement cycle.
Vehicle Processing Center (VPC)
DoD-operated facilities at major ports of embarkation/debarkation where members drop off and pick up their POV for overseas shipment. Major locations: Baltimore, Jacksonville, Houston, Los Angeles, Seattle, Honolulu.
